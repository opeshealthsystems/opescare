# 1. Purpose

OpesCare must not only be a software platform. It must also communicate clearly to the public.

The public website must help patients, hospitals, clinics, pharmacies, laboratories, insurers, developers, public health stakeholders, and partners understand what OpesCare is, how it works, how patient data is protected, how systems connect, and how organizations can participate.

The public pages must show that OpesCare is serious, transparent, safe, and built for Cameroon, Africa, and any health system that needs reliable digital health identity and connected medical records.

The website must be informative without being confusing. It must use clear medical language, not unnecessary jargon.

---

# 2. Public Website Goals

The public website must achieve these goals:

1. Explain OpesCare clearly.
2. Build trust with patients and families.
3. Show hospitals that OpesCare can improve continuity of care.
4. Show pharmacies how medicine availability can be synchronized.
5. Show blood banks and hospitals how blood availability and blood need requests can work safely.
6. Show labs how verified results can be connected to patient timelines.
7. Show insurers how claims and coverage can work with minimum necessary information.
8. Show developers how external systems can connect.
9. Explain privacy and patient data processing in plain language.
10. Provide clear terms, policies, and help content.
11. Support English and French from the beginning.
12. Present OpesCare as a world-class healthcare infrastructure product built by Opesware.

# 5. Visual and Content Rules for Public Pages

## Visual Rules

Use the OpesCare color and icon system.

Use Lucide icons only.

Do not use emojis.

Do not use cartoon doctors, random stock imagery, dirty icon packs, or decorative visuals that weaken trust.

Use clean healthcare imagery and interface mockups.

Preferred visuals:

- product interface mockups
- Health ID card illustration
- secure patient timeline graphic
- hospital-lab-pharmacy-insurer connection diagram
- consent approval flow
- API/integration diagram
- medicine availability map mockup
- blood availability network mockup
- security and audit diagrams

Avoid:

- fake smiling doctors with no product context
- crowded medical stock images
- overly dramatic emergency imagery
- fear-based visuals
- unrealistic medical claims

## Content Rules

Use clear medical language.

Avoid unnecessary jargon.

Explain technical terms when needed.

Never promise that OpesCare will prevent all medical errors.

Never promise that all hospitals will instantly share data.

Never imply that patient data is shared without consent or legal basis.

Never present medication availability as a substitute for medical advice.

Never present blood availability as something patients should handle alone.

Always communicate safety, consent, verification, audit, and professional medical supervision.

## Sample FAQs

### What is OpesCare?

OpesCare is a digital Health ID and healthcare interoperability platform that helps approved medical records follow the patient across healthcare providers.

### Is OpesCare a hospital management system?

OpesCare includes healthcare workflow modules, but its main purpose is broader: patient Health ID, medical history continuity, consent, and interoperability.

### Does the QR code show my full medical history?

No. The QR code should only contain a secure identifier or token. It does not reveal your full medical history by itself.

### Can any hospital see my records?

No. Access depends on authorization, consent where required, purpose of use, and policy.

### What happens in an emergency?

Approved providers may access a limited emergency profile when normal consent cannot be obtained. This access is recorded and reviewed.

### Can I see who accessed my record?

OpesCare is designed to provide access logs so patients can see when their record was accessed and by which facility or role.

### Can pharmacies update medicine availability?

Yes. Verified pharmacies and hospital pharmacies can synchronize stock so patients and providers can search for availability.

### Can I reserve medicine through OpesCare?

Where enabled, a patient may request a temporary reservation. Availability can change, so confirmation from the pharmacy is important.

### Can I use OpesCare to get blood directly?

No. Blood transfusion must be handled by qualified healthcare professionals. OpesCare can help authorized providers locate blood availability or coordinate requests.

### Can OpesCare connect to existing hospital software?

Yes. OpesCare can connect through APIs, SDKs, widgets, bridge agents, webhooks, and OpesCare Lite.

### Is OpesCare bilingual?

Yes. OpesCare is designed for English and French.
