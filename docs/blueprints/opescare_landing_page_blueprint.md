---

# 1. Landing Page Goal

The OpesCare landing page must explain the product clearly and convincingly to patients, hospitals, clinics, pharmacies, labs, insurers, public health stakeholders, and potential partners.

The page must make visitors understand this immediately:

**OpesCare gives every patient one secure Health ID that connects their medical history across hospitals, clinics, labs, pharmacies, insurers, and approved health systems.**

The landing page should not present OpesCare as just another hospital management system. It must position OpesCare as a **digital health identity and interoperability platform**.

The page must communicate:

- one patient, one Health ID
- medical records follow the patient
- hospitals can push and pull approved records
- patient consent controls access
- emergency access is possible but audited
- pharmacies and hospitals can synchronize medicine and blood availability
- OpesCare supports existing hospital systems through APIs, SDKs, widgets, and bridge agents
- the system is built for trust, continuity of care, and safer healthcare

# 22. Section 17: FAQ

## Purpose

Answer predictable questions.

## FAQ questions and answers

### Is OpesCare a hospital management system?

OpesCare includes hospital workflow modules, but its main purpose is bigger: it is a digital Health ID and interoperability platform that helps medical records follow the patient across approved healthcare providers.

### Can OpesCare work with existing hospital software?

Yes. OpesCare can connect through APIs, SDKs, widgets, bridge agents, webhooks, and OpesCare Lite for facilities without existing software.

### Can patients control access to their records?

Yes. Access is controlled through consent, purpose of use, role-based permissions, facility context, and audit logs.

### What happens in an emergency?

Approved providers can access a limited emergency profile when normal consent cannot be obtained. This access is reason-based, audited, and reviewed.

### Can pharmacies show medicine availability?

Yes. Verified pharmacies and hospital pharmacies can synchronize available stock so patients and providers can search for medicines more safely.

### Can hospitals show blood availability?

Yes. Verified hospitals and blood banks can publish controlled blood availability and blood need requests. Blood transfer must be handled by authorized healthcare professionals.

### Is OpesCare bilingual?

Yes. OpesCare is designed for English and French from the foundation.

### Does the QR code contain the full medical record?

No. The QR code should only contain a secure identifier or token. It does not expose the full medical history by itself.
